STEP 2: Build Your Public Narrative

Crafting your public narrative allows you to share your story—whether in a group setting or a one-on-one conversation. While you may not always have time to tell your full story, weaving elements of it into your presentation can make a big impact.

Public narrative is a framework that helps leaders inspire action. It consists of three key parts:

  • Story of Self: What personal experiences led you to care about voter registration in healthcare?
  • Story of Us: How does your story connect to shared values in the healthcare community?
  • Story of Now: What urgent challenge must we address together?

Refining your story takes practice. Keep these tips in mind:

  • Scars, Not Wounds – Share a story that reflects growth, not something unresolved.
  • Rooted in Values – Identify a core value that represents why this work matters to you.
  • Keep the Focus on You – Your audience wants to know *your* journey, not just others’ stories.
  • Make Them Feel – The best stories evoke emotion. Be authentic and vulnerable within your comfort zone.
  • Paint the Picture – Use sensory and emotive language to bring your story to life.

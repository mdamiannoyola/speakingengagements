STEP 2: Build Your Public Narrative

Crafting your public narrative allows you to share your story—whether in a group setting or a one-on-one conversation. While you may not always have time to tell your full story, you should find ways to weave elements of it into your presentation naturally.

Public narrative is a powerful framework that helps leaders communicate their values and inspire action. It consists of three key parts:

  • Story of Self: Why are you called to do this work? What personal experiences led you to care about voter registration in healthcare?
  • Story of Us: What values and experiences does your audience share? How does your story connect to the broader healthcare community?
  • Story of Now: What urgent challenge threatens these values? What action must we take together?
  • Scars, Not Wounds – Choose a story you are ready to share with colleagues. It should reflect growth and lessons learned, rather than an experience that still feels unresolved.
  • Rooted in Values – Identify a core value (or a mix of values) that truly represents who you are and why this work matters to you.
  • Keep the Focus on You – A common mistake is centering your story on others. While other people may be part of your journey, your audience wants to know who you are and what drives you.
  • Make Them Feel – The most impactful stories evoke emotion. Be brave and vulnerable (within your comfort level) to help your audience connect with your experience.
  • Paint the Picture – If you have the time to share a full narrative, use sensory and emotive language to make your audience feel like they were there with you.
